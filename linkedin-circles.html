LinkedIn Circles

Creating a better way to manage your LinkedIn connections.

Project Details

My Role:

Product Designer

Tools:

Figma

Duration:

2 Weeks

Problem

As my LinkedIn network grew, I realized that I couldn't remember why I was connected with many of my connections. This made me ask the question,

"How can I improve LinkedIn to better manage and organize my connections?"

Research

To gauge if others shared this experience, I conducted a poll on LinkedIn and discovered that 68% of the 77 respondents faced the same challenge.

Solution

To solve this problem, I designed a Figma prototype for a feature called LinkedIn Circles. This feature would enable users to privately group their connections, making it easy to remember how they know each person. To demonstrate this concept, the videos below show how Circles could be used in the context of a user's profile and on the My Network page.

I truly believe that for LinkedIn to effectively connect professionals, it needs a better system for organizing connections, and hope that incorporating a feature like this could be a significant step in that direction!

How to add and modify a Circle

Added a new menu option to the existing "more" dropdown menu to easily allow users to add a connection to a Circle. Additionally, clicking the Circle's tag would open a modal for users to view and edit the Circle.

Adding multiple connections to a Circle

For a more efficent process of adding your connections to Circles, I created a demonstration of how you might be able to add multiple connections from the My Connections page.
